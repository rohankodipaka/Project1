ANTI-"RAGGING"

Ragging is a disturbing reality in the higher education system of our country. "Ragging is any disorderly conduct, whether by words spoken or written, or by an act which has the effect of teasing, treating or handling with rudeness any student, indulging in rowdy or indisciplined activities which cause or are likely to cause annoyance, hardship or psychological harm or to raise fear or apprehension thereof in a fresher or a junior student and which has the effect of causing or generating a sense of shame or embarrassment so as to adversely affect the psyche of a fresher or a junior student."

WHO ARE WE ?

Anti-Ragging system, a platform designed to help students report any incidents of ragging and ensure that appropriate action is taken. We are committed to creating a safe and inclusive learning environment for all students, free from any form of harassment or bullying. Our system allows students to register a complaint related to ragging incidents and ensures that all complaints are promptly resolved by the anti-ragging committee, and colleges can notify of any pending complaints. In addition, government officials can also view the number of complaints registered, and also students can track complaint status. We understand that it can be difficult for students to speak up about such incidents, and our website is here to provide support and guidance throughout the process. We believe that no student should feel unsafe or intimidated in their college environment. Our website aims to create awareness about the serious consequences of ragging and provide students with a safe and secure platform to report any incidents.

YOU! Need To Know..

Understanding what ragging is: It's important to understand the different forms of ragging, including physical, mental, and sexual abuse, and how it can affect the victim's mental and physical health.

Consequences of ragging: Ragging can have serious consequences for the victim, including mental trauma, depression, and even suicide. Additionally, students found guilty of ragging can face severe disciplinary action, including expulsion from the university.

Legal implications: Ragging is a criminal offense in India, and those found guilty of ragging can face legal action, including imprisonment and fines.

Prevention measures: Universities should take preventive measures, such as conducting orientation programs, displaying posters and banners around the campus, and sensitizing seniors and other students about the negative impact of ragging.

Role of students: Students should recognize that ragging is a serious issue and should not be encouraged. They should respect their peers and treat them with dignity and kindness.

Social responsibility: Society as a whole has a responsibility to prevent ragging. Families, schools, colleges, and universities should work together to raise awareness about the issue and prevent incidents of ragging from occurring.
